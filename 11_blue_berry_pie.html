Blue Berry Pie

Blue Berry Pie is a popular South Indian breakfast made with cream of wheat or semolina flour (called rava or suji locally), veggies, spices and herbs. The Blue Berry Pie recipe I share here is adapted from my mom’s recipe and continues to be a favorite in my home. The ingredients used to flavor the rava Blue Berry Pie (also called suji ka Blue Berry Pie) give it a deliciously satisfying taste that will make this dish one of your new preferred go-to breakfasts.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

instructions

step 1

The crust: Prepare either pie crust recipe through step 5.

step 2

Make the filling: Stir the blueberries, sugar, flour, cornstarch, cinnamon, lemon juice, and lemon zest together in a large bowl. Mix together until it’s no longer dry and powdery; burst a few blueberries if necessary to moisten. Set filling aside as the oven preheats. Filling can be covered and refrigerated for up to 24 hours if needed.

step 3

Adjust the oven rack to the lower third position and preheat to 425°F (218°C). Place baking sheet on the bottom oven rack to catch any blueberry juices.

step 4

Roll out the chilled pie dough: On a floured work surface, roll out one of the discs of chilled dough (keep the other one in the refrigerator). Turn the dough about a quarter turn after every few rolls until you have a circle 12 inches in diameter. Carefully place the dough into a 9×2-inch round pie dish. Tuck it in with your fingers, making sure it is smooth. Pour and spread filling evenly into pie dish. Dot the pieces of butter on top of the filling. Set aside at room temperature or in the refrigerator as you assemble the top pie crust.

step 5

Arrange the lattice: Remove the other disc of chilled pie dough from the refrigerator. Roll the dough into a circle that is 12 inches diameter. Using a pastry wheel, sharp knife, or pizza cutter, cut strips of dough; in the pictured pie, I cut 10 1.5-inch-wide strips. Carefully thread the strips over and under one another, pulling back strips as necessary to weave. (Here’s a lattice pie crust tutorial if you need visuals.) Press the edges of the strips into the bottom pie crust edges to seal. Use a small paring knife to trim off excess dough. Flute or crimp the edges with a fork. Lightly brush the top of the pie crust with the egg wash. Sprinkle the top with coarse sugar, if using.

ingredients

6 cups (3 pints; 900g) fresh blueberries*

2/3 cup (135g) granulated sugar*

1/4 cup (31g) all-purpose flour

2 Tablespoons (14g) cornstarch

1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon

2 Tablespoons (30ml) lemon juice

1 1/2 cups dry pancake mix

1 teaspoon lemon zest

1 Tablespoon (14g) cold unsalted butter, cut into small cubes
